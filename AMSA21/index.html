Vision

Accessibility

Visibility, finances and opportunities for up-skilling are the three main pillars of AMSA involvement I hope to target through the year.

In an organisation this big, it’s no wonder it’s easy to feel overwhelmed - trust me, I’ve been there! I will make sure that all communication and call-out opportunities through the year come through to the Monash community in a digestible format.

I recognise that finances continue to act as a barrier to involvement, and will strive to work closely with MUMUS to advocate for greater financial support for students hoping to attend Council and NLDS. COVID has shown us that such events have the capacity to be successfully adapted to the online format, and it will be my priority to also push for the coordination of local satellite events to increase attendance accessibility.

With the success of the policy review teams and vampire cup year level coordinators this year, I will ensure that these opportunities for formal AMSA engagement at Monash continue to be available.

Advocacy

Your views are my priority. AMSA provides an incredible platform for discussion and change, and it would be my privilege to represent the Monash student body within discussions occurring in AMSA, MSCV (Medical Students Council of Victoria) and all other relevant organisations. The medical student voice holds great power that I hope to help harness in ensuring we are able to make positive change.

My inbox will always be open if you have suggestions or queries, and I strive to be someone you feel comfortable escalating issues to - whether it’s through your contributions at Think Tanks, or by dropping me a message over Facebook. AMSA policy is often described as the “beating heart of AMSA advocacy”, and I will be involved in discussions with the AMSA advocacy team to ensure the policy base covers the needs of Monash Medical students.

Events

A huge part of AMSA is the wonderful events it runs through the year! Although we’ve all missed out on boogie-ing with our interstate mates this year, I will ensure that 2021 is an absolute blast.

A barrier for events involvement is simply not knowing they exist, or not knowing the people involved. I hope to make AMSA events more accessible for all students, particularly our biggest event - National Convention - by partnering with MUKEG (Monash University Konvention Excitement Group) leaders to speak at the What’s-An-AMSA event held at the beginning of each year, and exploring similar engagement strategies for smaller AMSA events held through the year.

I know how hectic convention can be (in all its glory), but want to make it accessible and welcoming for all medical students, no matter previous AMSA involvement. Whether you’re attending for the academics or social nights, I will be there to look out for you.
